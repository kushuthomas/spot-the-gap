A two-minute exercise in AI judgment

Spot the Gap

The prompt looks fine. That’s the problem.

The request

Sent to an AI assistant

“I’m holding a workshop at a school and need to provide lunch. Please give me a grocery list with item quantities and I’ll order from the grocery store myself.”

Reads reasonably, right? But to actually fill this order, an AI would have to guess almost everything.

You probably caught
the loud ones.

How many people What kind of lunch Budget Ages of the group Kitchen equipment Timing Plates & utensils

Miss these and you get a bad lunch — wrong amounts, wrong food. Annoying, not dangerous.

The silent gap

Allergies &
dietary needs

Allergies & dietary needs

Nothing in the prompt hints at it, so the AI won’t feel it missing. It will cheerfully suggest peanut butter sandwiches and trail mix — a perfectly good answer, and at a school, a potential emergency.

The loud gaps cost you a lunch.
The silent gap can cost someone their safety.

And only a human knows the difference.

The AI didn’t guess wrong — it was never given enough to guess right.

It wasn’t the AI’s fault.
The prompt set it up to fail.

Strong prompts use six basic moves. This one skipped nearly all of them:

  1. 1Give context skippedNo headcount, no ages, no dietary info, no setting.
  2. 2Specify constraints skipped“Item quantities” can’t be met without the headcount.
  3. 3Show examples skippedNo sense of what kind of lunch.
  4. 4Define role & tone skippedCaterer? Nutritionist? Budget shopper?
  5. 5Break into steps skippedCan’t rescue a task whose core facts are missing.
  6. 6Ask it to think firstSame problem — thinking harder about missing facts doesn’t create them.

The real lesson

Not all missing
context is equal.

Most missing information is loud — the gap is obvious, and a careful person (or a good AI) will catch it. But the most dangerous gaps are silent: so obvious to the human that we forget to say them, and so invisible to the AI that it never asks.

Filling every blank isn’t the human’s job. Knowing which blank can hurt someone — that’s the job. That’s the part you can’t hand off.

Same request.
Rewritten.

Sent to an AI assistant — take two

“I’m hosting lunch for 30 middle-school teachers at an all-day workshop. There’s a fridge and microwave but no full kitchen. Please give me a grocery list with quantities for build-your-own sandwiches, including a vegetarian option, and keep everything nut-free. Budget is around $200.”

Now it’s answerable — and safe. Same person, same lunch. The only thing that changed was the human doing the thinking up front.
